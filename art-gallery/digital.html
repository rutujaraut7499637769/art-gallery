Digital art history can refer to two distinct fields: the history of digital art as an art form, or the field of digital art history, which uses digital tools to study art history . The history of digital art begins with early electronic experiments in the 1950s and 60s, evolving through pioneers like Ivan Sutherland with his Sketchpad program and Harold Cohen's AARON program, and accelerating with the advent of software like Adobe Illustrator and the growth of internet art

                                                                "Starry Night" digital art information refers to the digital versions and reproductions of Vincent van Gogh's 1889 oil painting, The Starry Night, which is now in the public domain and can be found in digital archives like the Google Art Project. Digital art inspired by Van Gogh's work often involves using software like Procreate to recreate the swirling sky, glowing stars, and flame-like cypress trees with digital brushes and layers. The original painting is a Post-Impressionist masterpiece housed at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City
