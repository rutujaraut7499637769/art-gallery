Abstract painting is an art form that does not attempt to represent reality realistically but instead uses visual elements like shape, color, line, and texture to evoke emotions or ideas. It emerged in the early 20th century and is considered a modern and contemporary style, with origins traced to movements like Cubism, Impressionism, and Post-Impressionism. The meaning is often open to interpretation, encouraging viewers to have a personal and emotional response to the work.

"Mystic forest" is a recurring theme in art, with many paintings depicting a tranquil and enchanting forest scene. These artworks often use shades of green, blue, and earth tones to create a sense of depth and serenity, and some feature elements like mist, moonlight, or a path to draw the viewer in. Popular examples include a watercolor painting by Ashish Sakat and an acrylic painting by Dhanshree Agrawal, highlighting that the term can refer to various styles and mediums.

                                                                "Urban Light" is a famous art installation by Chris Burden at the Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA), consisting of 202 restored 1920s cast-iron streetlamps arranged in a grid. This sculpture is also the subject of various paintings, both as a literal representation of the installation and as a source of inspiration for abstract art. The original "Urban Light" installation is a symbol of the city, and other "urban lights" paintings draw from the theme of cityscapes at night
